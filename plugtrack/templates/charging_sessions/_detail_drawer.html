Confidence Level Guide
What affects confidence?

Confidence levels indicate how reliable the efficiency and cost calculations are for a charging session. Lower confidence typically means the data is "noisy" or based on limited information.

High Confidence

Good data quality with sufficient window size and recent anchor points.

Medium Confidence

Some data quality issues that may affect accuracy.

Low Confidence

Poor data quality - calculations may be unreliable.

Common confidence issues:
  • Small Window Too little data: Sessions with <15 miles driven or <3 kWh delivered provide limited efficiency data.
  • Stale Anchors Old odometer readings: Anchor points more than 10 days old may not reflect current driving patterns.
  • Outlier Clamped Unrealistic efficiency: Values outside 1-7 mi/kWh are clamped as they're likely measurement errors.
