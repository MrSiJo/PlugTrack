Quick Start Guide

Get up and running with PlugTrack in just a few steps!

  1. Add your cars: Go to Cars and add your electric vehicles with their battery capacity and efficiency.
  2. Log charging sessions: Use the Add Session button to record your charging data.
  3. View analytics: Check the Analytics page to see your charging patterns and costs.
  4. Configure settings: Visit Settings to set up cost analysis and preferences.
Frequently Asked Questions

Adding a charging session is easy! Here's how:

  1. Click "Add Session": Use the blue "Add Session" button on the dashboard or go to the Charging Sessions page.
  2. Fill in the details:
    • Car: Select which vehicle you're charging
    • Date & Time: When did the charging start?
    • Start SoC: Battery level when you started charging (e.g., 20%)
    • End SoC: Battery level when you finished (e.g., 80%)
    • kWh Delivered: How much energy was added (check your car's display or charging station)
    • Cost: What you paid for this charging session
    • Location: Where you charged (home, work, public station, etc.)
  3. Save: Click "Add Session" to save your data.
Pro tip: You can also add sessions from the mobile app or import data from your car's app if supported.

Confidence is a measure of how reliable your charging session data is. PlugTrack analyzes several factors to determine data quality:

High Confidence
  • Complete odometer readings
  • Accurate charge amounts
  • Reasonable efficiency range
  • Recent data (within 7 days)
Low Confidence
  • Missing odometer data
  • Estimated charge amounts
  • Unusual efficiency values
  • Old or incomplete data
Why it matters: High confidence data gives you more accurate cost analysis and better insights into your charging patterns.

Learn More About Confidence

Cost analysis helps you understand the true cost of EV ownership compared to petrol cars. Here's how to set it up:

Step 1: Go to Settings

Navigate to Settings and click on the "Cost & Efficiency" tab.

Step 2: Set EV Efficiency

Enter your car's typical efficiency in miles per kWh (mi/kWh). This is usually between 3-5 mi/kWh for most EVs.

Step 3: Configure Petrol Comparison

Enter the current petrol price per litre and your previous car's MPG to compare costs.

Step 4: View Results

PlugTrack will automatically calculate:

  • Cost per mile for both EV and petrol
  • EV parity rate - the electricity price that makes EV and petrol costs equal
  • Savings analysis in your analytics
Real-time updates: The cost analysis updates automatically as you enter new charging sessions!
