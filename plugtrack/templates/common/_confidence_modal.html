Understanding Data Quality

Data quality levels show how reliable the efficiency and cost calculations are for your charging sessions. Lower quality usually means we have less information to work with, making calculations less precise.

Quality Levels
Reliable Data

Complete information available. Calculations are trustworthy.

Some Limitations

Most data available but minor issues may affect accuracy.

Limited Data

Several issues mean calculations should be taken with caution.

Common Data Issues
Short trip between charges
When you only drive a short distance between charges, efficiency calculations become less reliable.
Missing mileage reading
Without odometer data, we can't calculate how efficiently you're driving.
Very small charge amount
Small top-ups make cost and efficiency calculations less meaningful.
Long gap since last reading
If it's been a while since your last session, accuracy may be reduced.
Unusual efficiency reading
Sometimes calculations produce unrealistic results that get adjusted automatically.
Tips for Better Data Quality
  • Always record your odometer reading when logging sessions
  • Log sessions consistently, especially after longer trips
  • Avoid logging very small top-ups unless necessary
  • Check your readings for typos before saving
