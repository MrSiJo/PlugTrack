What Affects Confidence?

Confidence levels indicate how reliable the efficiency and cost calculations are for a charging session. Lower confidence typically means the data is "noisy" or based on limited information.

Small Window

When: Session delivers <3.0 kWh or drives <15 miles

Why: Small sessions provide less representative data for efficiency calculations

Stale Anchors

When: Reference data is >10 days old

Why: Older baseline sessions may not reflect current driving patterns or battery condition

Outlier Clamped

When: Calculated efficiency <1 or >7 mi/kWh

Why: Extreme values suggest measurement errors or unusual circumstances

Improving Confidence
  • Log longer charging sessions (more kWh and miles driven)
  • Keep your data up-to-date with regular session entries
  • Double-check odometer readings for accuracy
  • Note unusual circumstances (preconditioning, extreme weather) in session notes
